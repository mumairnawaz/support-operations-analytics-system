# 📊 Customer Support Performance & SLA Analytics System



## Background

Customer support teams handle large volumes of tickets daily, but without structured analytics, it becomes difficult to monitor performance, track SLA compliance, and identify operational bottlenecks.

This project analyzes customer support data to uncover inefficiencies in ticket resolution, SLA performance, and agent productivity.



## Executive Summary

This analysis evaluates **2,330 support tickets** to measure performance across ticket lifecycle, SLA compliance, and agent efficiency.

Key findings:

- Only **50% of tickets are fully closed**, indicating backlog issues  
- **974 SLA breaches** highlight delays in response and resolution  
- SLA compliance stands at **86.65%**, showing gaps in handling high-priority tickets  
- Email dominates support channels (~53%), while faster channels remain underutilized  

These insights reveal operational inefficiencies impacting response times and customer satisfaction.



# Performance Insights



## Ticket Volume & Trends

Ticket volume spikes during peak months and working hours, while after-hours support capacity remains limited.

**Insight:**  
Backlogs are driven by high ticket inflow without proportional staffing.

## SLA Compliance Analysis

SLA performance shows delays in both first response and resolution timelines.

- **86.65% first response compliance**  
- **974 SLA breaches identified**

**Insight:**  
High-priority tickets are not consistently meeting SLA targets.

## Ticket Status & Backlog

Ticket status distribution highlights inefficiencies:

- 50% closed  
- 31% in progress  
- 17% open  

**Insight:**  
A large number of in-progress tickets indicates resolution delays and process bottlenecks.

Agent-level analysis shows performance variability:
